Napoleon's childhood

The early years of the young Napoleon Bonaparte, the future emperor of France.

Childhood

Born in Corsica in 1769, Napoleon Bonaparte came from a family of Italian nobility. His father, Carlo Buonaparte, was a lawyer who was appointed as Corsica's representative at the court of Louis XVI, while his mother, Maria Letizia Ramolino, was known for her firm discipline.

His father, Carlo Buonaparte, was a lawyer and Corsican representative at the court of Louis XVI, and his mother, Maria Letizia Ramolino, was known for her strict discipline, which strongly influenced Napoleon's character.

Napoleon's Academic Background

Napoleon was baptized a Catholic and began his education at a theological school, to learn French. In May 1779, he was admitted to the Military Academy at Brienne-le-Château, where he particularly excelled in mathematics.

In 1784, he was admitted to the prestigious École Militaire de Paris, which he graduated in just one year, due to intense effort and the difficult family situation after the death of his father. At the age of only 16, he became a second lieutenant in artillery.

During the French Revolution, Napoleon returned to Corsica, where he supported the Jacobin faction. He participated in local political and military conflicts, and his pro-republican pamphlet, "Le Souper de Beaucaire", brought him acclaim and promotion to brigadier general after his success at the Siege of Toulon.
